Hydrogen Safety Leadership Initiative

Uniting organizations committed to world-class hydrogen safety.

A tiered recognition framework for organizations demonstrating increasing levels of commitment, leadership, transparency, and excellence in hydrogen safety.

Explore the Framework

Initiative Overview

A maturity journey, not a compliance checklist.

The Hydrogen Safety Leadership Initiative rewards genuine community contribution, operational learning, and sustained improvement. Each tier builds on the last, giving organizations a clear pathway to grow from foundational participation to recognized industry leadership.

Three Recognition Levels

Recognition grows with commitment, contribution, and evidence.

Tier 1

H2 Safety Champions Program

Commitment & Transparency

Entry-level recognition for organizations establishing foundational hydrogen safety culture and practices.

  • Executive endorsement of hydrogen safety principles
  • Public or internal hydrogen safety commitment statement
  • Designated hydrogen safety lead or responsible manager
  • Participation in hydrogen safety training and education
  • Use of recognized hydrogen safety guidance and standards
  • Basic incident reporting and near-miss tracking
  • Annual lesson learned, case study, near miss, or best practice submission

CHS membership encouraged

Tier 2

Hydrogen Safety Vanguard Circle

Leadership & Contribution

Advanced recognition for organizations actively shaping the hydrogen safety community through mentorship, collaboration, and technical leadership.

  • Formal hydrogen-specific process safety management
  • Hazard analysis and risk assessment processes
  • Hydrogen competency programs for relevant personnel
  • Contractor and supply chain safety expectations
  • Regular sharing of incidents, lessons learned, or operational insights
  • Presentation at CHS or industry events
  • Participation in peer mentoring, site exchanges, working groups, or committees

Active CHS membership required

Tier 3

Hydrogen Safety Platinum Recognition

Excellence & Performance

The highest level of recognition for organizations demonstrating world-class safety performance, independent validation, and sustained industry stewardship.

  • Multi-year record of strong hydrogen safety performance
  • Mature safety management systems and executive-level leading indicators
  • Third-party assessment, audit, or peer review
  • Influence on industry standards, codes, and technical resources
  • Published case studies or technical contributions
  • Demonstrated just culture and learning culture principles
  • Cross-functional safety integration across the organization

CHS membership, sustained leadership, and documented contribution required

Program Pillars

Consistent criteria across all tiers.

Leadership & Governance

Executive accountability, safety ownership, and formal organizational commitment to hydrogen safety culture.

Safety Management Systems

Structured processes for hazard identification, risk assessment, and hydrogen-specific process safety.

Workforce Competency

Training programs, contractor expectations, and verified hydrogen safety knowledge across relevant personnel.

Operational Learning & Transparency

Incident reporting, near-miss tracking, lesson sharing, and a commitment to open organizational learning.

Community Contribution

Active engagement in CHS activities, working groups, peer mentoring, and industry knowledge advancement.

Continuous Improvement & Innovation

Demonstrated improvement cycles, adoption of emerging practices, and pilot programs for proactive risk reduction.

Design Principles

Credible, inclusive, and mission-aligned.

Mandatory elements are paired with flexible leadership criteria, allowing organizations to demonstrate maturity through meaningful contributions such as case studies, mentoring, conference presentations, working groups, peer review, or guidance development.

Renewal Structure

  • Champions: Annual affirmation of continued commitment
  • Vanguard: Requalification every 2–3 years with documented evidence
  • Platinum: Requalification every 2–3 years with third-party validation
Recognition is based not on the absence of incidents, but on demonstrated commitment to learning, transparency, and continuous improvement.

Membership Requirements

Balancing broad engagement with meaningful leadership recognition.

Champions — Encouraged

CHS membership is encouraged but not required, maximizing inclusion across the hydrogen ecosystem and creating a broad pipeline into higher tiers.

Vanguard — Required

Active CHS membership is required because this tier is fundamentally about contributing back to the hydrogen safety community.

Platinum — Required + Active Contribution

CHS membership is required, along with active tenure and documented contributions to the hydrogen safety knowledge base.

Strategic Rationale

Organizations recognized as hydrogen safety leaders should actively contribute to advancing hydrogen safety for the broader community. That is defensible, mission-aligned, and central to CHS’ role as a convening body for shared learning.
